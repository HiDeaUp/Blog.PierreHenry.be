Having a vision for your life changes everything. When you know exactly where you want to go, when you have a clear idea of who you want to become in five or ten years, your daily decisions start to align with that future. It’s not just about setting goals; it’s about picturing yourself, your identity, and the person you want to grow into.

Let’s say you’re currently a senior software engineer. If you see yourself becoming a lead developer or even a CTO, you naturally start to take on more responsibility at work. You behave more like a senior, you look for opportunities to manage others, and you get involved in the bigger picture of your company. Over time, you build up new skills and knowledge, and when you move to a new company, you’re ready for roles like manager or lead engineer. Step by step, you get closer to your vision.

This approach isn’t just for your career. If you’re building a startup, having a clear vision of what you want it to become helps you move steadily toward that goal. The same goes for your personal life. Combining your professional and personal visions, and making sure they fit with your values, leads to a more fulfilling life.

Life is too short to just go through the motions. You should love your life and live it to the fullest. The way to do that is by creating a lifestyle that matches your values and what you enjoy. The more you identify what truly makes you happy, the more you can bring those things into your daily routine.

For example, maybe you realize that spending all day in front of a computer isn’t healthy or enjoyable. Striking a balance between work and personal life is much more pleasant. You might decide to add more exercise or travel to your life. Personally, travel is a real passion for me. Sometimes, spending extra money to fly business class on long trips is worth it because it makes the experience so much better. On a ten-hour flight, I find I’m much more productive and comfortable, which makes a difference.

Money comes and goes, but time only moves forward. If you spend hours in discomfort just to save a little, you’re losing time you can never get back. Life is like a train that never stops. It’s important to make the most of your time and fill your days with moments you enjoy. A collection of good days adds up to a beautiful life.

> *Life is a train that never stops. Make sure you enjoy the ride.*
— Pierre

If you want to go even further in improving your daily life, I recommend keeping a notebook. I have a favorite one myself, and it’s a great way to track your vision, your progress, and the things that make you happy. Writing things down helps you stay focused on what matters most.

*TL;DR / Key Takeaways:*
- Having a clear vision for your future shapes your daily choices.
- Align your lifestyle with your values and what brings you joy.
- Time is more precious than money; spend it on what matters.
- Balance between work and personal life leads to greater happiness.
- Use a notebook to track your vision and progress.

*Ask Yourself:*
- What does your ideal future look like in five or ten years?
- Are your daily actions moving you closer to that vision?
- What small changes can you make today to live a life you truly enjoy?
