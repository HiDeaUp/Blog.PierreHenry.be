Have you ever felt like you’re always surrounded by noise, stress, and endless distractions? It’s so easy to get caught up in the daily grind, with notifications popping up and people interrupting just when you’re about to get something important done. That’s why I want to share a simple but powerful idea: creating your own Safe Space.

A Safe Space is more than just a quiet room. It’s an environment where you can be completely with yourself, free from interruptions, and able to focus on what truly matters to you. Think of it as your personal bubble, where stress and distractions are left at the door.

For example, right now I’m sitting in a small meeting room. The door is closed, and there’s a sign outside that says I’m busy. It’s a bit like a radio studio when the “On Air” light is on—nobody dares to disturb. You can do the same at home. Put up a simple sign on your door, like the “Do Not Disturb” cards you see in hotels. The moment you hang it up, everyone knows not to interrupt. You could even create your own version, something that signals to others that you’re in your Safe Space, focused and working on your own projects.

Being with yourself and doing things just for you is not selfish. It’s essential. It’s about giving yourself the chance to concentrate, to create, and to achieve things that matter to you. I really encourage you to set up your own Safe Space—a spot that’s just for you, where you know no one will distract or disturb you. It’s your time, your place, and that’s all that matters.

If you want to go even further and boost your productivity, I’ve developed a method called Deep Immersion. It’s all about finding ways to get more done while actually feeling happier and more fulfilled. Too often, when we try to do more, it feels like we’re in the army, under strict discipline and pressure. This method is the opposite—it’s about working deeply, but with joy and satisfaction.

I truly hope your Safe Space helps you as much as it’s helped me. Don’t put it off. Don’t tell yourself, “Maybe I’ll do it later.” Start today. Look around your home or apartment and find a spot you can transform into your own Safe Space. It doesn’t have to be big or fancy—just a place where interruptions and stress are left outside, and you can finally focus on what matters to you.

## Key Takeaways
- A Safe Space is a dedicated spot where you can be with yourself, free from distractions and interruptions.
- Use a simple sign or signal to let others know you’re not to be disturbed.
- Don’t wait—choose a place in your home today and make it your own.
- Focusing on yourself is not selfish; it’s necessary for real progress and happiness.

## Action Steps
- Pick a room or corner in your home to be your Safe Space.
- Create a “Do Not Disturb” sign or signal.
- Set aside regular time to use your Safe Space for your personal projects or reflection.

## Reflection
*What’s stopping you from creating your own Safe Space? How would your life change if you had a place to focus just on you?*
