Setting challenges for yourself can truly change your life. When you create your own goals, even small ones, you add a spark to your daily routine. It’s like adding a pinch of salt and pepper to a bland dish—suddenly, everything has more flavor. You can use reminders, apps, or even a simple notebook to keep track of your challenges, but the most important thing is to have something that excites you.

Having both long-term projects and short-term goals gives you a reason to jump out of bed in the morning. Imagine waking up on a Saturday at 6 a.m. with a personal project you’re eager to finish in three weeks. That feeling of excitement can make your whole day brighter. On the other hand, if you have nothing planned, you might wake up, look at the clock, and go back to sleep. Before you know it, it’s noon and you feel like you’ve wasted half your day. That’s when the day starts off on the wrong foot.

This need for purpose is not just a personal opinion—it’s a basic human truth. Think about people in retirement homes. After the holidays, when their loved ones have visited and left, many feel like they have nothing to look forward to. It’s sad, but often, after the festive season, some lose their will to live. They feel alone, waiting another year for the next visit. This shows how important it is for everyone to have a reason to wake up, to feel alive, and to keep going.

Having projects and goals is what keeps us happy and even helps us live longer. Even something as simple as reading a book can keep your mind awake and give you a sense of purpose. Sure, not every book is a masterpiece, but the act of reading itself is stimulating. That’s what I wish for you—to find things that keep you awake and excited about life.

## Key Takeaways
- Set personal challenges to add excitement to your days.
- Mix long-term projects with short-term goals for daily motivation.
- Even small activities, like reading, can keep your mind sharp.
- Time is precious—don’t waste it waiting for the “right” moment.
- The best time to start is always now.

## Reflection
- What challenge can I set for myself this week?
- How can I add more purpose to my mornings?
- Am I making the most of my time, or am I letting it slip away?
