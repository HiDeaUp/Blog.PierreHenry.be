Have you ever felt that your life is good, but not truly fulfilling? Maybe you sense there’s more out there, something richer, more exciting, but you’re stuck in the same old routines. We all have our habits and rituals, and changing them overnight is tough. It’s not easy to wake up one morning and decide to become a different person. That’s why, sometimes, the best way to move forward is to wipe the slate clean and rebuild everything from the ground up.

This idea isn’t just about making small tweaks. It’s about completely breaking down your habits and rituals to create new foundations. If you can, change your environment radically. Move to a new country, or at least to a different city. If that’s not possible, take a week—better yet, three weeks—in a hotel somewhere far from your usual surroundings. Even if it’s just a town nearby, the key is to get away from your daily triggers and routines.

If you have the means, set aside some savings for this. Three weeks in a hotel might sound extravagant, but think of it as an investment in yourself. During this time, cut off contact with your usual circle. Don’t tell everyone you’re wiping the slate clean—just say you’re taking some time for yourself. The goal is to be in a completely different environment, free from the habits and expectations that usually shape your days.

Most of our lives are run by habits. Some are neutral, like brushing your teeth or looking in the mirror. Others are less helpful, like checking your reflection forty times a day out of insecurity. We often don’t even notice these patterns. Recording yourself for a day can be eye-opening. You’ll spot little tics and words you use without thinking. Try banning a word for a day, or even speaking in a different language for a few hours. These small experiments force you out of your comfort zone and make you aware of your automatic behaviors.

The most fertile ground for change is right after you’ve cleared everything away. It’s like a field that’s just been plowed—ready for new seeds. When you wipe the slate clean, you give yourself the chance to start fresh, as if you were born again. Try flipping your routines: have dinner for breakfast, breakfast for dinner, or do your evening rituals in the morning. Live as if your world is upside down, just for a while.

One week isn’t enough. It takes at least three weeks for your brain and body to adapt to new habits. Remember, you only have one life. Why not try living it differently, even if just for a short time?
