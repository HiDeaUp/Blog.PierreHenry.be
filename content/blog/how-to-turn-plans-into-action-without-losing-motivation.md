When it comes to planning what you want to achieve, there are countless ways to go about it. Whether it’s a passion, a hobby, or a dream you’ve been holding onto for years, the first step is always the same: start small and build from there.

Imagine laying the first stone of a building. Today, you can do just that by picturing what you want to create in your life. Bit by bit, as you keep this vision in mind and take small steps every day, you’ll see yourself moving closer to your dream. If you dedicate even just an hour or two each day, after a month or two, you’ll start to see real results. This is the power of the compound effect.

But here’s the catch: if you only work on your goal today, then wait three days, then two weeks, you’re relying on motivation alone. Motivation is unreliable. It comes and goes, and if you depend on it, you’ll find it much harder to stick with your plans over the long run.

Instead, you need a system. Something solid. Discipline is what keeps you moving forward, step by step, thanks to your routine. When you lean on your routine, not your motivation, you make progress every day. Your routine becomes automatic. Maybe after your shower, you work on your project. Maybe it’s after breakfast. The key is to make it part of your daily life, so it becomes second nature.

Routine is what carries you, not motivation. When your actions become automatic, you don’t have to think about them. You just do them. That’s how you turn your plans into reality.

> *Success is the sum of small efforts, repeated day in and day out.*
> — Robert Collier

---

## Key Takeaways

- *Start small and build your dream step by step.*
- *Rely on routine, not motivation, to make progress.*
- *Make your actions automatic by tying them to daily habits.*
- *The compound effect will help you achieve big results over time.*
