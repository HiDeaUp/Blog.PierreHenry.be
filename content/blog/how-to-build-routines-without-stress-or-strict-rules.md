When was the last time you truly let yourself relax? Not just sitting on the couch, but really allowing your mind and body to unwind. We often talk about routines and organisation as if they’re strict rules to follow, but what if the real secret is learning to let go, even just for a few minutes?

Let me share a simple exercise that can change your day. Try standing up and bouncing gently on the spot. It might sound silly, but it’s a trick that even Tony Robbins uses to boost his energy. You can also play with your voice, letting it slip and slide in different directions. It’s a bit like freestyle dancing, where anything goes. These moments of play are more than just fun—they’re a way to shake off stress and reconnect with yourself.

There’s even a World Laughter Day in France, where people gather just to laugh together. It might look odd from the outside, but laughter is a powerful tool. When you laugh, it’s almost impossible to feel sad at the same time. Try it: laugh out loud, even if you’re alone. Notice how your mood shifts. As Corine, a laughter coach, shows in her videos, it’s about letting go and feeling good, even if there’s a cat or a dog wandering in the background.

Breathing is another simple way to reset. Take a deep breath, fill your belly, hold it for five to seven seconds, then let it out slowly. This kind of breathing calms your mind and brings you back to the present. It’s a form of meditation that doesn’t need any special equipment or training.

Have you ever woken up from a dream with a wild idea or a new perspective? These moments come when your mind is relaxed and open. That’s why it’s so important to make space for relaxation in your daily routine. Sometimes, just being barefoot on a wooden floor can help you feel grounded and connected to the world around you. It’s a small thing, but it makes a difference.

The real goal in life isn’t just to be organised or productive. It’s to feel good, to be at peace with yourself, and to enjoy the world around you. When you’re relaxed, you’re more creative, more motivated, and more ready to take on new projects. If you feel tired or unmotivated, it might not be because you’re doing too much. Maybe you’re missing that spark—a project or passion that excites you.

Remember, life is shorter than we think. We all have the same 24 hours in a day, but we never know how many days we have left. Don’t wait for the perfect moment to start something new. Take action now, even if it means making mistakes along the way. Mistakes are the best teachers.

It’s easy to fall into the trap of endless planning and learning without ever doing. The real progress comes from putting ideas into practice. At the end of each day, take a moment to look back. Ask yourself: How did my day go? If something didn’t go well, think about what you can change tomorrow. Improve your days by just one or two percent, and over time, you’ll see a big difference.

## Key Takeaways
- Relaxation is just as important as organisation.
- Simple exercises like bouncing, laughing, and deep breathing can boost your mood and energy.
- Being barefoot or connecting with nature helps you feel grounded.
- Review your day and make small improvements regularly.
- Take action now—don’t wait for everything to be perfect.

## Reflection
- When was the last time you truly relaxed?
- What small routine could you add to your day to feel more at peace?
- Are you taking action on your ideas, or just thinking about them?
