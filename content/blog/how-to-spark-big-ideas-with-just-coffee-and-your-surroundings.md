There’s something special about sitting in a cozy café, sipping a cappuccino, and watching the world go by. Today, I found myself at a spot in King’s Cross, a place known for its vegan and gluten-free treats. The weather isn’t great, but the view of the street lined with trees makes up for it. I often come here to study, and lately, I’ve been learning the R programming language. If you’re into data science or want to get better at analytics, R is a solid choice.

But what really struck me today wasn’t the code on my screen. It was the way this place inspires me. Every environment speaks to us in its own way. For me, this café sparks ideas and helps me see things differently. Maybe for you, it’s somewhere else—a park, your living room, or even a quiet corner at work. The important thing is to soak in your surroundings. The more an environment “talks” to you, the more creative connections you’ll make. Sometimes, that’s all you need to solve a problem you’ve been stuck on for ages.

We all hit roadblocks—at work, in relationships, with family, or just in life. You might be struggling with something and can’t seem to find the answer. Then, out of nowhere, you’re in a place where you feel relaxed and at ease, and suddenly, it clicks. You realize what you need to do, what choice to make, or how to move forward. It’s funny how these moments often come when you’re not even thinking about the problem. You’re just letting your mind wander, and then—boom—you get that spark.

I call these moments “brainstorming sessions,” but they don’t have to be formal. Sometimes, they happen in a jacuzzi, a bath, or a sauna. I love hotels with saunas for this very reason. These are the times when I get my best ideas, whether it’s for a new project, a big life decision, or even just a poem. The key is to give yourself space to breathe and let your thoughts settle.

I encourage you to set aside time for yourself like this. Think of it as a date with yourself—a chance to step back and reflect on your life. These moments of calm can lead to breakthroughs that change everything. You might realize what you truly want, what you need to do next, or even start a whole new chapter. Maybe you’ll decide to write a book in Scotland, spend time in an Irish castle, or try something completely new in Japan. It could be anything—a martial art, a poem, or just something that makes you happy.

What matters is finding what lifts you up. I hope you find your own inspiring spot, wherever that may be.
