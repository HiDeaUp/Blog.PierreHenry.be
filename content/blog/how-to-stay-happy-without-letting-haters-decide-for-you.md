When you start a new project, especially if you’re creating something like an information product, you quickly notice two types of people. There are those who support you, and then there are those who just don’t like what you do. Sometimes, these critics are loud, and it’s easy to feel their words more than the encouragement from your supporters.

Here’s the thing: most of the time, you don’t even know these people who criticize you. They might say harsh things, but that’s just how it is. It’s tough to accept, but it helps to keep an open mind. Maybe some feedback is genuine, maybe it’s just a difference in generations, but in most cases, their negativity brings you nothing useful.

It’s much harder to focus only on the positive comments. In fact, it can be so challenging that some people hire a virtual assistant to filter messages and only show the good ones. You don’t have to manage every email or comment yourself. Let someone else handle the noise, so you can stay motivated by the support you receive.

Sometimes, the best thing you can do is take a break. Go on vacation, clear your mind, and stop paying attention to the haters. There will always be critics, especially online. If you let them get to you, they win. But why should you let people you don’t even know decide how you feel or whether you keep chasing your dreams?

You need to protect yourself. The more haters you encounter, the more you build up a kind of immunity. If you let their words stop you from doing what you love, they win again. And all because of strangers whose opinions shouldn’t matter to your life.

Don’t let your dreams fade away because of people you’ll never meet. Focus on those who support you. It’s not easy, but it’s important. With practice, you’ll notice the difference. Over time, you’ll get better at ignoring the negativity and appreciating the encouragement.

## Key Takeaways
- Most haters are strangers whose opinions don’t matter.
- Focus on positive feedback and support.
- Take breaks when needed to protect your mental space.
- Practice ignoring negativity—it gets easier with time.

## Reflection
- Are you letting strangers decide how you feel about your work?
- What could you achieve if you only listened to those who support you?
- How can you protect your dreams from outside negativity?
