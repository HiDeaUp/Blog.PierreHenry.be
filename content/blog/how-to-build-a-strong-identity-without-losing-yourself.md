Have you ever stopped to think about who you really are? Not just your name or your job, but the core of your identity. Defining your identity is much more than a philosophical exercise. It’s the foundation that supports your goals, your habits, and the direction of your life.

When you have a clear sense of who you are, everything becomes easier. You know what you want, what your long-term goals are, and why you do what you do every day. Sure, building good habits helps. Spending twenty minutes a day learning programming, marketing, or working on your side project is great. But if you add a strong identity to the mix, you’ll find it even easier to stay on track.

Think about it: if you know your strengths and weaknesses, you can make better choices. For example, if you realize that drinking too much with friends leaves you feeling terrible the next day, you can decide to drink water instead. It’s not just about willpower; it’s about knowing what’s good for you and what isn’t.

Here’s a simple exercise: imagine your name as an entry in the dictionary. What would you want it to say? If I opened a dictionary and saw my own name, Pierre-Hory, what would I want the description to be? This exercise is powerful because it forces you to think about the person you truly want to become.

Once you have that vision, you can compare it to your current habits. Are your daily actions helping you become that person, or are they holding you back? If there’s a gap, maybe it’s time to change some habits.

Ask yourself how you want to define yourself, both personally and professionally. Don’t set any limits. Deep down, what do you really want? This is your chance to shape your own definition, not just for others, but for yourself.

## Key Takeaways

- Defining your identity makes it easier to set and reach your goals.
- Good habits are important, but a strong sense of self makes them stick.
- Imagine your name in the dictionary—what would you want it to say?
- Compare your current habits to your ideal self and adjust as needed.

## Action Steps

- Take ten minutes to write down how you would describe yourself in a dictionary.
- Identify one habit that doesn’t match your ideal identity and change it this week.
- Reflect on your strengths and weaknesses to make better daily choices.

## Reflection

*Who do you want to become? What would your dictionary entry say? Are your habits helping you get there, or is it time for a change?*
