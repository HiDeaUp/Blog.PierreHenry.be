Excellence is not some distant goal reserved for a select few. It is a way of moving forward, a mindset that pushes you to go further each day. If you take a moment to look at what you can do, what you can change—especially what you can change in your daily routine—you can improve yourself by at least 1% every single day.

James Clear, in his book *Atomic Habits*, talks about this idea. He reminds us that only a fool keeps repeating the same actions and expects a different life. If you keep doing what you have always done, your life will not change. To build something new, you have to change, you have to try new things, and see if there are other ways to do what you do.

This is where having an analytical mind comes in. Take the time to observe yourself. At the end of each day, do a quick review: How did my day go? What went well? What did not? This kind of daily retrospective helps you improve. It lets you put things in place so that your mistakes or the things that make you unhappy happen less often, and the things that make you happy or work well can be maximized.

If you want to build a different life, you have to be willing to look at yourself honestly and make small changes, one step at a time. Excellence is not about being perfect. It is about being a little better than yesterday.

## Key Takeaways

- Excellence is about daily improvement, not perfection.
- Review your day to see what worked and what did not.
- Small changes, made consistently, lead to big results over time.
- Try new ways of doing things if you want a different outcome.
