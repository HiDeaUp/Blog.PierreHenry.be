Have you ever noticed how some mornings you wake up craving a coffee or a croissant, eager for a comforting breakfast? These little rituals are not just habits; they are part of your personal well-being routine. When you focus your energy on your well-being, especially on what feels most central to you—your happiness—everything else tends to fall into place more easily.

The best advice I can give is to make your well-being a priority. Take time to step back and identify the things that truly contribute to your sense of happiness. Sometimes, it’s as simple as a warm croissant or a fresh cup of coffee. These small pleasures can genuinely lift your mood, even if, in the long run, a croissant every day might not be the healthiest choice. The key is to find comfort foods and routines that are also good for your health. For example, if you love kiwis or bananas, why not blend up a banana milkshake with yogurt? It’s delicious and nourishing.

The real secret is to look for the things that make you happy and to notice them. You can even track your moments of happiness: rate your enjoyment from 0 to 10, ask yourself what you liked about the moment, and think about what could have made it even better. What could you add to your day to turn a good day into an amazing one? Step by step, you’ll find yourself not only happier but also radiating more positive energy.

The more you feel good, the more you’ll spread that positive energy to others. It’s contagious. Imagine going to the bakery and the person behind the counter barely smiles at you. It doesn’t make your day any brighter. But if you go somewhere where people are kind, pay attention to details, and remember your favorite order, it changes everything. That small act of kindness can lift your spirits and improve your whole day.

If everyone took care of their own happiness and well-being, the world around us would feel lighter and more welcoming. Never underestimate the power of your own happiness to influence those around you.
