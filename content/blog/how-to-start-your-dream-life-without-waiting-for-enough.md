Many people believe they need to be rich or have plenty of money before they can start working on their dreams. This idea holds so many of us back. The truth is, waiting for the perfect time or the perfect amount of money is just another way to delay what really matters to us.

If you look around, you’ll notice that it’s easier than ever to split payments or pay in installments. Whether it’s online services or even in stores, you can often spread out the cost without paying extra fees. Sometimes, you don’t even pay any interest. This means you can start now, instead of waiting for years to save up every penny.

The real problem isn’t how much you earn. It’s about taking the first step toward what you want. If your dream is to travel, open a bakery, or start DJing, you’ll never find the perfect moment. Life always throws new problems your way. There will always be bills, unexpected expenses, or reasons to put things off. The “right time” rarely arrives on its own.

It’s a bit like people who say they’ll only retire when they have a certain amount saved up. Most of the time, that moment never comes. The same goes for your dreams. If you keep waiting, you’ll always find another excuse. You have to make space for your dreams yourself—no one else will do it for you.

I’m not telling you to max out your credit card. But if you know you can pay something off over time, and you have a steady income, maybe it’s worth considering. The key is to find a way to start, even if it’s small. Maybe you need to cut some expenses, move somewhere cheaper, or use the internet at the library instead of at home. There’s always a smarter way to do things if you really want it.

Sometimes, we convince ourselves that something is impossible, but there’s always a solution if you look for it. Everything is possible if you’re willing to take the first step. Don’t wait for the stars to align. If you keep putting things off, there will always be another problem down the road. The perfect moment doesn’t exist—you have to create it.

If you keep telling yourself, “I can’t do it because I don’t have this or that,” you’ll never get started. So, stop postponing. Lay the first stone of your project today. Take one step, then another, and soon you’ll see progress. It’s like compound interest: small actions add up over time and lead to big results.

> *The best time to plant a tree was twenty years ago. The second best time is now.*
>
> — Chinese Proverb

Don’t let excuses hold you back. If you think you can’t make a video or a podcast because it’s too noisy or you don’t have the right equipment, you’ll never do it. Start with what you have, even if it’s not perfect. If you want to make videos but don’t have a fancy camera or microphone, use what you’ve got. If it works and you get some traction, you can always improve later.

The most important thing is to start. Don’t wait for the perfect conditions. Begin with what you have, and step by step, you’ll get closer to your dream. I’m rooting for you.
