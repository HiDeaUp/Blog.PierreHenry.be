Having a clear vision of where you want to go in life is important. But sometimes, what really keeps you moving forward is having a side project—a small passion project that lights you up, even if it’s not the most profitable thing you could do.

What matters most is that this project gives you energy and feeds you every day. Maybe you love sharing your knowledge, or you have a deep passion for writing. Publishing books isn’t easy, and it doesn’t always bring in a lot of money. Still, you can write and publish your own books, and even teach others online how to write theirs. You might offer courses on writing, and at the same time, keep editing and publishing your own work. This way, your passion becomes more rewarding, both personally and financially.

Sometimes, you just need to tweak your passion a little to make it more profitable. For example, if you love crafting, drawing, or making beautiful photo albums, you might not earn much just by selling your creations. But you can create courses for people who want to learn these skills. Platforms like Podia or FastSpring make it easy to sell these courses. Even if you don’t have a huge audience, having a loyal group of students can make your project worthwhile.

The most important thing is to be truly happy with what you do. When you’re passionate, you naturally do better than your competitors. That passion becomes your biggest advantage. Even if you don’t have a massive following, your genuine love for your work will help you make a living from it.

> *The most important thing is to be truly happy with what you do. When you’re passionate, you naturally do better than your competitors.*
— Pierre

If you want to go even further in improving your life, remember that there are always new ways to grow and learn. I’m rooting for you, and I wish you all the best on your journey.

## Key Takeaways
- *A side project you love can keep you motivated, even if it’s not the most profitable.*
- *You can often turn your passion into something more rewarding by teaching others.*
- *Happiness in what you do is more important than money—passion gives you a real advantage.*

## Reflection
- *What small project truly excites you, even if it doesn’t seem profitable?*
- *How could you share your passion with others or teach them your skills?*
- *Are you choosing happiness and meaning over just chasing profit?*
