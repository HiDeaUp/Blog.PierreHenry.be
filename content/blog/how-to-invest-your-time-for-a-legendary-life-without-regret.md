Have you ever stopped to think about how you spend your time? Not just in the big moments, but in the small, everyday choices that add up to your entire life? Sitting here in a hotel room in Melbourne, I realized something simple yet powerful: most of us don’t invest enough in our time. We let it slip by, often without noticing, and then wonder why we feel stuck or unfulfilled.

Time is like a train that never stops. If you waste five minutes, especially on something that brings you nothing—like a boring movie or a pointless task—you can never get those minutes back. Imagine spending an hour on something that doesn’t make you happy, doesn’t teach you anything, and doesn’t even entertain you. That’s an hour lost forever. Instead, you could have used that hour to do something that brings you joy, helps you grow, or even earns you money. That’s how you start to build a better, freer life.

Sometimes, you might have to do things you don’t love, like jogging or a coaching session, but if that hour brings you a reward—say, 5,000 euros—it opens up new possibilities. You could treat yourself and your loved ones, travel, or invest in your own growth. The key is to see these moments as investments, not just in money, but in your future happiness and freedom.

There are many ways to invest in yourself. It’s not always about getting money back right away. Sometimes, it’s about spending more to use your time better. For example, paying for a nicer hotel room might seem like a luxury, but if it helps you use your time more wisely, it’s worth it. The real question to ask yourself is: “Do I like what I’m doing right now?” If the answer is yes, great. If not, it’s time to do something else. Life is too short to waste on things that don’t matter to you.

Being aware of how you spend your time is crucial. If you feel like you’re wasting your life, don’t wait—take control and make a change. Question yourself often. This is how you move forward, make better decisions, and learn from your mistakes. Don’t be afraid to make mistakes. They are the stepping stones to higher levels. If you never make mistakes, you’ll always stay on the ground floor. You need mistakes to reach the 10th, 12th, or even 20th floor of your life.

Mistakes are how you learn. But there’s a difference between passive learning—just watching videos or reading without doing anything—and active learning, where you try, fail, and try again. The real growth happens when you take action, even if it means crossing a desert of doubt and difficulty.

There will be times when you feel lost, like you’re in the middle of a desert with no end in sight. But often, just when you’re about to give up, you notice signs of hope—a bird, a shell, a distant boat. That’s when you realize you’re closer than you think. Most people give up right before they succeed. But if you keep going, even when things are tough, you’ll reach your destination.

> *“The only limit that exists is the one you create for yourself.”*  
— Pierre

There’s never a perfect time to start. If you wait for the right moment, you’ll be waiting forever. Five years from now, you might have even less time, less money, and more responsibilities. The best time is always now. Don’t put off your dreams. Take action today.

Believe in yourself. Change your perspective if you need to. Sometimes, the only thing holding you back is your own mindset. If you want something badly enough, you’ll find a way. Be persistent. Don’t be afraid to ask for what you want. If you don’t ask, the answer is always no. But if you do ask, you open the door to new possibilities.

> *“Not asking is the same as getting a no.”*  
— Pierre

This applies to everything, even how you talk to yourself. If you focus on negative thoughts, your days will reflect that negativity. Instead, act like the main character in your own life. Tell yourself, “Everything is going to be fine. I’m not a victim—I’m the actor of my life.” This mindset changes everything.

Don’t be afraid of rejection. Most of the time, the worst that can happen is a no, which is what you already have if you don’t ask. Whether it’s asking for an upgrade at a hotel, a free dessert at a restaurant, or a new opportunity at work, just ask. You’ll be surprised how often the answer is yes.

> *“You never get what you deserve, only what you negotiate.”*  
— Pierre

Always ask yourself positive questions. Instead of worrying about what could go wrong, ask what would happen if everything went right. What if the answer was always yes? This opens up a world of possibilities.

So, take charge of your life. Invest in your time, ask for what you want, and never settle for less than you deserve. The legendary life you dream of is within reach—if you start now.
