When we think about happiness, many of us imagine it as a race. We picture a long track where the winners are those who collect the most degrees, land the highest-paying jobs, and climb to the most prestigious positions. Maybe you dream of launching a startup, selling it for a fortune, and then doing it all over again. For many people, this is what success looks like.

But is that really what happiness is about? I want to offer a different perspective—one that focuses on well-being and living a life that feels good, not just one that looks impressive from the outside.

Imagine waking up in the morning feeling comfortable, not just financially, but in your own skin. You don’t have to worry about skipping meals or denying yourself a night out at a restaurant. You live in a decent home, but you don’t need a million or a billion in the bank just for the title of “billionaire.” Instead, you have a life where you might work at a job you enjoy or even live off your passion. Most importantly, you have time for yourself—to read, to write, to go to the movies, or even binge-watch films all night if you feel like it. If you want to take a vacation and relax in a jacuzzi or treat yourself to a spa day, you can do that too.

A fulfilling life isn’t about working yourself to the bone just to stack up money or impress people you don’t even care about. Too often, people chase after diplomas or high salaries just to gain approval from others, sometimes even from people who aren’t friends or who don’t matter to them at all. We get caught up in wanting to look successful, but forget to ask ourselves if we actually feel happy.

True success is about living a life that excites you, a life that makes you feel alive deep down. It’s about having a mission or a passion that gets you out of bed on Monday morning with the same excitement you feel on Friday night. When your work feels like a passion, it brings in enough money for you to live well, but you’re not obsessed with earning more just to show off.

Sometimes, we chase after status or degrees to impress others, but in the end, it doesn’t bring real happiness. What really matters is feeling fulfilled in your daily life. There are so many other things that can bring you joy and satisfaction.

Another important point is the influence of your environment and the people around you. The moods and attitudes of those close to you can affect your own well-being, both positively and negatively. It’s worth taking the time to look at your surroundings and relationships, and see how they impact your happiness.

> _Happiness is not something ready made. It comes from your own actions._
>
> — Dalai Lama

> _Too many people spend money they haven't earned, to buy things they don't want, to impress people they don't like._
>
> — Will Rogers

Life moves quickly. Don’t put off living the life that excites you. Start now. Build a life that speaks to you and makes you enthusiastic every single day.
