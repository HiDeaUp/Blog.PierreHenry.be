Sometimes, you just have to go. You grab your coat, put on your headphones, and get ready to leave. It might sound simple, but this small act holds a powerful lesson: your time and priorities matter, and you have every right to protect them.

How often do you find yourself stopped in the street by someone asking for a survey or a quick chat? Maybe it’s a friendly face, someone who seems genuinely nice. Still, you have a train to catch, an appointment to keep, or something important to do. In these moments, it’s okay to say, “I’m in a hurry, I have to go.” You are not being rude; you are respecting your own priorities.

It’s easy to fall into the habit of always pleasing others, of saying yes to every request, even when it means putting your own needs last. But if you always put yourself second, it catches up with you. Over time, you might start to feel drained, less happy, or even resentful. You might notice you’re less patient or kind with others, simply because you haven’t taken care of yourself first.

The truth is, when you are happy with yourself, you have more to give to others. Taking care of your own priorities is not selfish—it’s the foundation for being able to give your best to the people around you. Your priorities count. What you want to achieve in your day matters.

So next time someone asks for your time and you know you need to move on, remember: it’s okay to say no. It’s okay to put yourself first. Your time comes before everything else.

## Key Takeaways

- Your time and priorities are important—protect them.
- Saying no is not rude; it’s necessary for your well-being.
- Pleasing everyone else at your own expense will only hurt you in the long run.
- When you take care of yourself, you have more to give to others.
