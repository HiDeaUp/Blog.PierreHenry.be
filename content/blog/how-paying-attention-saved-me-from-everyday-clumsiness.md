Sometimes, a simple act of carelessness can feel like a big mistake. Let me share a story that might sound familiar. One day, I placed my phone on a stool. It was just a quick move, nothing special. The floor looked a bit dirty, so I grabbed a cloth to wipe it. As I cleaned, I noticed there was also some leftover food under the stool from the previous night’s dinner.

Without thinking much, I lifted the stool to clean underneath. That’s when it happened. My phone, which I had forgotten was on top, slipped and crashed to the floor. It could have been avoided so easily. I had put my phone on the stool, then got distracted by cleaning, and forgot all about it.

This kind of thing happens to all of us. We get caught up in small tasks and forget the obvious. The real lesson here is about attention. If I had been more mindful, I would have remembered where I put my phone. It’s not just about being careful, but about being present in the moment and predicting what might happen next.

Paying attention is a basic principle, but it’s easy to overlook. We often think we can multitask or rush through things, but that’s when mistakes happen. By slowing down and focusing on what we’re doing, we can avoid many little accidents and frustrations.

> _The difference between ordinary and extraordinary is that little extra._
— Jimmy Johnson

> _Attention is the rarest and purest form of generosity._
— Simone Weil

---

## Key Takeaways
- Small acts of carelessness can lead to bigger problems.
- Being present and attentive helps prevent avoidable mistakes.
- Slow down and focus on one thing at a time.

## Action Steps
- Before moving objects, check what’s on or around them.
- Take a moment to notice your surroundings before starting a task.
- Practice being present, even in simple daily routines.

## Reflection
*When was the last time a small distraction led to a mistake? What could you do differently next time?*
