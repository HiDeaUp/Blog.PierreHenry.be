We often talk about happiness, well-being, and self-improvement. But today, let’s look at something a bit different: how to become a better version of yourself, not by comparing yourself to others, but by comparing yourself to who you were yesterday. The real challenge is to make sure that tomorrow, you’re a little better than today.

## Start With a Clear Action Plan

The first step is to have a plan of action. Picture yourself a year from now. What is your main goal? Where do you want to be? Imagine your future self, not the process to get there. If you focus too much on the steps, you might get discouraged and give up before you even start. Instead, see the final version of yourself. That’s your target.

Once you have that vision, work backwards. Like rewinding a movie, trace the steps from your future self back to where you are now. Write these steps down in a notebook. Break your big goal into smaller pieces, like slicing a cake. Each slice is a step you need to take.

For example, if you want to earn €10,000 a week, break it down by seven days, then by the number of hours you want to work. Ask yourself: What do I need to do each hour? What can I offer my clients? What can I automate? How can I earn money even while I sleep? Maybe by creating a website or a service, or by delegating tasks to others.

## Use the Power of Technology and Delegation

Today, technology makes almost anything possible. You can find services for everything with a simple Google search. Need IT support? Want to hire a manager for your café or restaurant? There are platforms like Upwork, PeoplePerHour, and others where you can find qualified freelancers for marketing, management, or development.

Of course, you might need some capital to get started. But you can also start from scratch. Earn a bit by making videos, writing a book, or selling software. If you want to learn programming, there are free bootcamps like School 42, founded by the creator of Free Telecom. These schools are now in Paris, San Francisco, and many other cities. They’re free, full-time, and open to anyone who wants to learn.

If you’re not ready for that, there are countless free tutorials on YouTube and other sites. The only thing stopping you is excuses. Excuses only hold you back. Don’t let them. Go for it. I believe in you, truly.

## The Power of Small Daily Improvements

The real secret is to improve a little every day. This is the compound effect: each day, do something a bit better than the day before. Over time, these small steps add up to big changes. That’s how you reach your ultimate goal.

> _Success is the sum of small efforts, repeated day in and day out._
>
> — Robert Collier

> _Don’t compare yourself to others. Compare yourself to the person you were yesterday._
>
> — Anonymous

## Key Takeaways

- Visualize your future self and work backwards to map out the steps.
- Break big goals into small, manageable tasks.
- Use technology and delegation to save time and energy.
- Avoid excuses—take action, even if it’s just a small step today.
- Improvement is about doing a little better each day.

## Action Steps

- Write down your one-year goal.
- Break it into weekly and daily steps.
- Find one thing you can automate or delegate this week.
- Learn something new for free online.
- Track your daily progress in a notebook.

## Reflection

*What small step can you take today to be better than yesterday? What’s stopping you from starting right now?*
