Frustration is something that follows us everywhere. It sneaks into our days, often because we carry a cloud of negativity, fear, or worry. This energy shapes how we experience life. Maybe you regret not waking up earlier, or you feel bad about time you think you’ve wasted. You might look back and think, “I could have done this,” or “I should have made that decision.” Before you know it, hours and days slip by as you replay these regrets in your mind.

This is where the real danger lies: feeding your days with negativity. The more time you spend stuck in regret, the less time you have to actually change your life. Instead of asking yourself why you lost that time or why you didn’t make a different choice, focus on what you can do right now. The past is over. You can’t go back. What matters is turning the page and moving forward.

It’s easy to get caught up in the feeling of frustration, letting it bubble inside. But the most important thing is to keep moving. Life is short. The real question is: what can you do today that will make a difference for your future? Use your time wisely. The past is gone, but you can still make good decisions now.

I wanted to share these thoughts because everyone faces frustration and negativity. We all feel that energy inside us sometimes. But you have to remind yourself: yes, things happened a certain way, but you can keep going. That’s what counts most—moving forward.

> *Life is short. The past is gone, but you can still make good decisions now.*

There’s only one life. Don’t keep putting off what you want to do. Take action now. Make today the day you lay a new stone on the path of your life. The right decision is always the one you make now, not the one you keep waiting for.

## Key Takeaways
- Frustration comes from holding onto regrets and negativity.
- The more you dwell on the past, the less you move forward.
- Turn the page, focus on today, and take action now.
- Life is short—use your time to make choices that matter.

## Reflection
- What regrets are you holding onto that you could let go of today?
- How can you use your time better, starting now?
- What small step can you take today to move forward?
