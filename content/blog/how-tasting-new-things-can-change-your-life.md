Sometimes, we all crave something delicious. Maybe it’s a piece of chocolate or a new dessert you’ve never tried. These cravings are not just about food—they’re about wanting to experience something different. Just as we might want to taste a new treat, we also long for new experiences in life.

Trying new things, especially when it comes to travel, is like giving your mind and body a fresh flavor. When you visit places like Thailand, Vietnam, or Costa Rica, you’re not just seeing new sights. You’re creating new life experiences. These moments do more than fill your memory—they open your mind and teach you things you never knew existed.

Meeting new people in different countries can teach you lessons you’d never learn at home. You discover customs, habits, and ideas that are completely new. This openness makes you more creative and helps you see the world in a different way.

Travel also pushes you to be more productive. When you’re in a new place, you have to adapt. Sometimes, you take on jobs or challenges you wouldn’t have considered before. These opportunities can lead you to places—both in your career and in your life—that you never imagined.

All these new experiences are like adding new ingredients to your life’s recipe. They help you do more, see more, and become more. That’s why I talk about productivity in this context. It’s not just about doing more work, but about living more fully.

One important thing: don’t just try to do this—do it. The word “try” sends a signal to your brain that you might fail, as if it’s not really possible. But it is possible. I believe in you, and I wish you all the best from the bottom of my heart.

> *The real voyage of discovery consists not in seeking new landscapes, but in having new eyes.*
>
> — Marcel Proust

## Key Takeaways
- Tasting new foods is a metaphor for seeking new experiences.
- Traveling and meeting new people opens your mind and teaches you unexpected lessons.
- New experiences can make you more productive and lead to opportunities you never imagined.
- Don’t just try—do it. Believe in your ability to change and grow.
