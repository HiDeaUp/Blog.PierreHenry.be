If you want to build something solid and become independent, the worst thing you can do is wait for the perfect moment. Don’t wait until you feel ready, or until you have a company or a fancy structure. You can start right now—just by publishing content and building an audience. That’s actually the most important step, even more than thinking about selling right away.

If you try to sell online to strangers who don’t know you, it’s going to be tough. Why would someone buy from you if they have no idea about the quality of what you offer? It’s hard to trust something you can’t see behind the scenes. Unless you have a product that solves a painful problem and really stands out, selling to people who don’t know you is an uphill battle.

Instead, focus on creating content and building a movement around your cause, your work, or your ideas. Even better, make your content spark curiosity—give people a reason to wait for what you’ll do next. It’s a bit like creating a sense of rarity, even if it’s only virtual. This is called “planned scarcity.” Think about diamonds: they aren’t actually that rare, but because a single family controlled most of the diamond factories, they could make diamonds seem rare and valuable.

It’s the same with housing: when there are fewer places to live, prices go up because demand is higher than supply. If what you create has little demand, it’s the opposite of rare. What you want is huge demand and limited availability. You can create this feeling, or at least stimulate it, by making your work seem rare—not by lying, but by being honest and genuine, and by treating your audience and clients like VIPs.

Remember when flying was a luxury? Only a few people could afford it, and the experience was unique. Now, everyone can fly, and it’s just another way to get from A to B. The glamour is gone. Today, space travel is the new VIP experience—rare, expensive, and unforgettable. The same goes for a trip to Antarctica: it’s rare and special, so people pay a lot for it. But flying on a plane? Not special anymore.

You don’t want to be like today’s airlines—just another company doing the same thing as everyone else. You want to be unique, the one that nobody can replace. Your know-how, your way of presenting your ideas, your products, and the way you treat your audience—this is what makes the experience truly special and authentic.

> *Don’t be just another cow in the field—be the purple cow that everyone notices.*
— Seth Godin

Sometimes, you also need to innovate in your business model. What you show on social media isn’t always your real business model. Maybe you make most of your money from upsells or coaching, not from the main product you sell at a low price. Maybe your main product is just a way to get known, and the real income comes from what you offer next.

For example, you might create online courses that don’t make much money directly, but they help you build a reputation. Then, people get to know you and are willing to pay for coaching or more advanced help. That’s where your real business is.

The best time to take action is today. Don’t put off what you want to do. Start by building an audience, not by trying to monetize them right away. Feed your audience with valuable, high-quality content in a specific niche. Step by step, people will start talking about you and following you.

Choose the right platform for your audience—TikTok and LinkedIn are very different, for example. Once you know where you want to build your audience, you can start growing from there. Later, you can set up a company, sell products, and so on. But it always starts with building an audience and standing out.

> *The best moment to start is now. Don’t wait for tomorrow to do what you can do today.*
— Anonymous

## Key Takeaways

- Don’t wait for the perfect moment—start now by publishing content and building an audience.
- Focus on creating value and curiosity, not just selling.
- Make your work feel rare and special by being genuine and treating your audience well.
- Innovate in your business model—sometimes the real value is in what you offer after the first sale.
- Choose the right platform for your audience and grow from there.
