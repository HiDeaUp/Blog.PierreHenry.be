We all want those “happy moments”—times when you feel truly good, at ease, and in tune with yourself. But what if happiness isn’t just about waiting for those moments to appear? What if you could actually build them into your day, every single day?

Let’s talk about the power of routine. Not the boring kind, but the kind that gives you a gentle start and sets the tone for everything that follows. Imagine waking up in the morning and knowing exactly what you’ll do first. For me, living in Melbourne, it’s simple: I wake up, drink two glasses of room-temperature water (yes, there’s a reason for that—I explain it in my book), and then I head to a coffee shop just around the corner for a good coffee. That’s my morning ritual.

Why water first? Because it wakes up your body gently, about fifteen minutes after you get up. It’s a small act, but it makes a big difference. After that, maybe you go for a short jog, or just take a moment for yourself before the day really begins. If you have an hour or two in the morning, before work or before any obligations, that’s your time. In cities like Melbourne, Brussels, or Paris, maybe you take the tram to work. But from 6 to 8 am, you can have a “recharge moment”—a time to feel good, to get into your own rhythm, to do something that matches your needs.

This could be a small project, or even just investing ten minutes a day in yourself. Watch a short video, learn something new—after a week, that’s already over an hour spent on your personal growth. Ten minutes a day may not sound like much, but it adds up. And the morning is the best time for it, because you never know what the day will bring. There will be surprises, mental fatigue, and things that drain your energy. That’s why it’s so important to start your day with something that’s just for you.

When you wake up, ask yourself: What do I want to do today? What do I want to achieve? This simple question can change everything. For me, it’s been a real boost—a moment that helps me feel better and more invested in the rest of my day.

The real secret? Regularity. Going to bed at the same time, waking up at the same time, and making a little space for yourself every morning. Each day is a new chance to move closer to the person you want to become.
