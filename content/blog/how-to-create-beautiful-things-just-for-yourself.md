Have you ever felt the urge to do something that truly excites you? There’s a special kind of joy in creating things for yourself—projects that make you happy and help you grow. These are the things that add real value to your life, whether you share them on your resume, add them to your portfolio, or simply keep them as a source of pride for your family, your ancestors, your partner, or your children.

When you work on something that matters to you, you get that “wow” feeling. It’s the sense of being proud of yourself, of leaving a positive mark that lasts. Imagine waking up on a Monday morning with the same excitement you feel on a Friday. You get up early, maybe at 6 a.m., because you can’t wait to start on your project. Even if you only spend half an hour a day, doing it regularly will lead to real progress.

It’s a bit like sculpting a stone. Each day, you chip away, and over time, you see the shape of your work appear. These projects leave a trace—a positive signature of who you are. They give you a kind of reputation, a unique identity, just like a signature drink at a bar. Your projects become your signature in your field.

You might introduce yourself by what you’ve created: “I’m Pierre Henry, author of this book,” or “I’m Pierre Henry, creator of this course,” or “I’m Pierre Henry, founder of this startup.” Maybe you’ve developed an algorithm that’s widely used, or an open-source software that helps others. These achievements give you character and help you stand out in your field in a positive way.

What’s most important is consistency. You don’t need to spend hours every day. Just half an hour, done regularly, is enough. The key is to keep going, to be steady in what you do. That’s what I do, and I encourage you to do the same, from the bottom of my heart.

*TL;DR / Key Takeaways:*
- Do things that make you happy and proud, just for you.
- Even small daily efforts add up to something big over time.
- Your projects become your signature and help you stand out.
- Consistency is more important than intensity.

*Actions List:*
- Pick one project that excites you and start today.
- Dedicate just 30 minutes a day to it.
- Track your progress and celebrate small wins.

*Ask Yourself:*
- What is one thing I can start today that will make me proud tomorrow?
- How can I make time for my own projects, even if it’s just a little each day?
