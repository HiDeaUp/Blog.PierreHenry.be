Many of us have dreams and goals. Maybe you want to launch a start-up, start a partnership, or open a small restaurant. But there’s something that holds us back: confidence. We often don’t see ourselves actually doing the thing we dream about. It feels out of reach, so days, months, and even years go by, and nothing changes.

Here’s the truth: there will never be a perfect moment. Waiting for the stars to align is just another way to stay stuck. Taking action is a bit like jumping into a pool from a diving board. You hesitate at first, but once you leap, you’re in. That’s how it works with your dreams too.

You will make mistakes. That’s not just normal—it’s the only way forward. Mistakes are not disasters. They are simply signs that you are trying, searching, and learning. If you want to avoid mistakes completely, you’d have to stay in bed all day, every day, for your whole life. But that’s actually the biggest mistake of all.

Doing nothing is the only real failure. When you take action, you’re not making mistakes—you’re learning. If something doesn’t work out as planned, it’s not a failure. It’s a lesson. For example, if you start a business and it doesn’t make enough money, you might have to close it. That’s not a mistake. You tried, you learned, and maybe the business model wasn’t right. So you pivot. You try a new idea.

This is how progress happens: by iterating, making new versions, and trying new things. Step by step, you get closer to what you could achieve.

I know it’s not easy. I’m right there with you. But please, start something today and finish it—even if it takes a year. Always finish what you start. Of course, don’t be stubborn if something clearly isn’t working. Ask yourself if you can pivot or try a different approach. Maybe you were building an app to scan food products, but it’s not catching on. You could shift to scanning alcoholic drinks instead, or find another angle. You can reuse what you’ve already built and keep moving forward.

The important thing is to keep going, to keep learning, and to keep trying. That’s how you make your dreams real.

> _The only real mistake is the one from which we learn nothing._
— Henry Ford

> _You don’t have to be great to start, but you have to start to be great._
— Zig Ziglar

## Key Takeaways
- There is no perfect moment to start—just start.
- Mistakes are part of the process; they mean you’re learning.
- Doing nothing is the only real failure.
- Pivot and adapt if things don’t work out as planned.
- Always finish what you start, but don’t be afraid to change direction.

## Reflection
- What’s one thing you can start today, even if you don’t feel ready?
- Are you waiting for confidence, or are you willing to build it by taking action?
- How can you turn your next “mistake” into a lesson?
