When you build something every day, you’re not just making progress—you’re developing what feels like a magical power. Creating daily, whether it’s a side project, a business, or simply nurturing a passion, is about more than just productivity. If you become obsessed with your project, you naturally rise above the average. You start to see connections others miss, and your mind becomes a playground for new ideas.

The more you immerse yourself in your chosen field, the more expertise you gain. This isn’t just about repetition. It’s about living and breathing your craft until you see things nobody else does. That’s the real magic: being constantly surrounded by creation, which leads to fantastic results.

Let’s say you spend time every day in a cozy café. Over time, you notice the details—the cushions, the lighting, the atmosphere. If you ever decide to open your own café, you’ll already have a list of ideas, preferences, and improvements in mind. You might even keep notes in your phone, building a future business plan without even realizing it.

This applies to any project. If you dream of opening a restaurant, and you visit different places every day, you’ll develop a sixth sense for what works and what doesn’t. You’ll notice the little things that make a place special, and you’ll start to imagine how you’d do it differently. Bit by bit, you weave together knowledge and expertise, setting yourself apart from the crowd.

Over time, this obsession makes you an expert. Your value skyrockets because you know so much more than the average person. You become the go-to person in your field, the one who can solve problems others can’t even see. Your hourly rate goes up, not just because you ask for it, but because you truly deliver more value.

There’s a book I remember—its title is something like “How to Become Outstanding” or “How to Be Irreplaceable.” The idea is simple: develop such rare and deep expertise that nobody can replace you. You become unique, the person who knows their subject inside and out.

Think of it like the difference between a general practitioner and a specialist doctor. The specialist charges more and has a long waiting list because everyone wants their expertise. They can diagnose problems that others miss because they know their field so well.

You can do the same. Use tools like artificial intelligence to sharpen your skills and become even more effective. Find your niche, your area of expertise, and excel at it. That’s where your magical power lies.

> *If the path is beautiful, don’t ask where it leads. Just enjoy the journey.*
> — Anonymous

When you’re passionate about what you do, you don’t worry about where it’s taking you. You’re not just working to pay the bills. Sure, money matters, but what really drives you is the excitement and satisfaction you get from your craft. That’s what makes you better than the rest—you’re not doing it out of obligation, but out of genuine love.
